I want to be an optimist about technology. Built properly, the internet can make life better. But "built properly" is a big If, and lately that If is doing a lot of work.

Lately I keep noticing dark patterns: tricks apps use to make you do things you didn't mean to. The term's from designer Harry Brignull, whose [Deceptive Design](https://www.deceptive.design/) catalogues them. His [Hall of Shame](https://www.deceptive.design/hall-of-shame) tags 25 examples to Facebook, 12 to Instagram, 7 to Meta. Still: 37.

Meta's the prime example, and Instagram's the one under my skin. You can follow accounts as fast as your thumb moves, but try to unfollow more than a handful and you hit a wall, or get locked out. Their business is ads, so anything that helps you leave costs them. These days they barely show your follows anyway, mostly "suggested" junk.

So why stay? The people. Friends and family are all here, the one thing I can't script around. If they moved somewhere better I'd be gone in a heartbeat.

Fourteen years in, I've followed 4,945 accounts I now want gone. Unfollowing by hand is the busywork obstruction is built to make you quit, and accept the status quo. So I had Claude write a script. From my phone, in Claude Code. Fuck yeah.

It worked. Ten accounts in, locked. Ran it again: 112, no lock. Again: locked after five. Same input, different result every time. Every run's a roulette wheel, so I'll keep spinning it.

The tech to give me a clean feed exists, it'd take Meta an afternoon. They won't, because serving me doesn't serve the ad machine. Built like this, it mostly makes Zuckerberg richer, money he spends buying up Hawaii (2,300-plus acres of Kauai, even taking locals to court over their land). That's where our scroll time goes. Fuck that.

Scripts are on GitHub: [ggfevans/insta-unfollow](https://github.com/ggfevans/insta-unfollow). Only 4,833 to go... assuming Meta doesn't lock me out first.
